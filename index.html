My Skills

Part of what makes us unique are our values, what is important to us, what we are prepared to take a stand on. We feel stressed or angry when we make choices that are not in line with our values because we know that we know that we are not really 'doing the right thing' for us. Articulating our values helps us make choices that are right for us.
This exercise is done in 2 steps.
Step 1
Fairly quickly and spontaneously, go through each of the skills and drag and drop them under the appropriate column headings according to how much you enjoy using that skill. Don't overthink, go with your instincts. There are no right or wrong answers, just what's right for you.

Step 2
Now sort each column into the rows according to how competent you feel you are at each skill. You're not comparing yourself to an expert. Think of it with regards to ‘am I better than most people from the general public at this?’ If the answer is ‘yes’ then drag and drop that skill into the row 'Better than most people’. Don’t be modest! If you’re unsure, give yourself the benefit of the doubt.

We are happier at work when we are spending most of our time using skills that we love and are good at in the top left corner of the grid.
